What is PAANN T2D Browser?

PAANN T2D Browser is a tool designed to help researchers explore SNPs associated with Type 2 Diabetes (T2D), specifically in the South Asian population. It allows users to access SNP information by entering an rsID, gene name, or chromosomal coordinates. The browser also displays various statistical analyses and visualisations, giving users a comprehensive view of their SNP of interest.

Features

  • Search SNPs by rsID, gene name, or genomic position
  • View SNP-related annotations, associations and mapped genes
  • Visualise statistical analyses and download respective results
  • User-friendly interface for easy navigation

Our Mission

Our mission is to provide a user-friendly platform for genetic researchers studying Type 2 Diabetes. By integrating various data sources, we aim to enhance the understanding of risk factors associated with T2D.

Our Motivations

The motivation behind the development of PAANN T2D Browser is to address the lack of tools that focus on SNPs associated with Type 2 Diabetes in the South Asian population. With much emphasis placed on research for different ancestries, we hope to bridge this gap and contribute to the growing pool of genetic data for this population, furthering advancements in T2D research.
